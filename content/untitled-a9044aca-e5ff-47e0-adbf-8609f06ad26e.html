Dijkstra’s Algorithm

Problem Statement:This algorithm deals with the problem of finding a path of minimum total length between two points say P and Q.

Approach:

Fact 1: If a point R lies on the minimum length path from P to Q (say minpath), then length of the path from P to R along minpath is the minimum length path from P to R.


   

Fig: Assume that PRQ is the minimum length path from P to Q but PR is not the minimum length path of R from P.Let the minpath for R be via <<x>>.PQ=PR+RQ.So new minpath of Q from P would be <<x>>+RQ and PR> <<x>> so <<x>>+RQ is less than PQ but that contradicts the fact that PQ is minpath for Q.Hence the assumption of having <<x>> as minpath is wrong and the above fact holds true.

Thus all points or nodes lying on the minpath from P to Q have their respective minimum length paths from P along minpath.

Thus a possible solution to finding minpath is to find minimum length path of each node in the graph till the destination node is reached.


Starting with initial case:




In this case, minimum length path from P to Q, would be shortest direct branch from P and Q and so R coincides with Q. Thus minpath for R is 1 as so is the minpath for Q.

 

Case 2: If no. of nodes in graph>2(say 3) and R lies on minpath from P and Q.

 


The solution’s approach is to find minimum distance to R from Based on fact 1,minimum path from P to R will be through some R’ lying on minpath from P to R. R’ is any node other than origin node(ie P). Only R remains.Thus R is R’ and minpath to R’ or R is 1.Based on the assumption that R lies on minpath from P to Q and minpath to R already calculated,for distance between P and Q to be minimum,distance from R to Q should also be minimum.Now the bigger problem of finding minpath from P to Q reduces to a smaller problem of finding minpath from R to Q.

Again assume some R’ (other than R) lying on minpath from R to Q .Only Q remains. So minpath for R’ or Q is the shortest branch from R to Q. ie 1.Summing both the minpaths (i.e. of R and R’), minpath for Q is 2.

 

Case 3:

Total no. of nodes in graph >3 and R lies on the minimum length path from P to Q.




The first step is to find minpath of R .R can be any node other than P. In our graph, R can be R1,R2,R3 or Q. Since we are standing at P and can look only as far the nodes that are immediately connected to P, we only know the distances to those nodes adjacent to P and rest are at an infinite distance from P. In selecting R, we apply a greedy approach and select that node as R which is nearest to P. In our case it is R1.So minpath of R1 becomes known as 2.Minpath of R3 is 3 and minpath of rest as infinite.In the previous case after R,only Q was left to qualify as R and after computing its minpath,algo terminated.Here,after minpath to R1 is calculated,next node to qualify as R will be selected based on their current distance from P.So now,R2’s dist from P is 3,R3’s is 3 and Q’s is 7.Choose R2 as next R and repeat the process.now dist of Q from P becomes 5 which replaces 7 as the lesser value while that of R3 remains 3. Repeat till all qualified candidates of R have been traversed and only Q is left.After R3 becomes R,new distance to Q from P comes as 11 but previous value 5 is less and hence minpath to Q from P comes as 5.The nodes of minpath can be known by traversing backwards and thus minpath becomes PR1R2Q.  Thus this recursive approach can be extended to a bigger network having any number of nodes in between P and Q.The time complexity for algorithm is O(|V|2) where V is the number of vertices.

 

Conclusion: To conclude,Dijkstra’s Algo uses this recursive method of finding of minpath by breaking down the system of bigger network into smaller sub networks and calculating the minimum path using principle of optimality i.e. Fact 1 as mentioned above.
