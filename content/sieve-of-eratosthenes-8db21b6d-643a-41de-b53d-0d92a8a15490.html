Sieve of Eratosthenes                              

(Akshit Agarwal, 10103534)

Algorithm:

It’s used to find all the prime numbers up to any given limit. It does so by iteratively marking as composite the multiples of each prime, starting with the multiples of 2.


Approach that might be used for solving the problem:

  • Problem was to find the number which are not divisible from any other number except themselves and 1.
  • Now mostly people at that time use to think that start from the 2 and check that if that number is divisible by any number smaller than it. And possibly these simpler algorithms would already been invented but they were time taking.
 
  • There are very good amount of chances that the inventor of sieve algorithm would had thought that we just have to find out the numbers which are divisible by 1 & themselves and not the other numbers, so why to check all the numbers.
 
  • Then he might have thought that when we don’t have to check the numbers which are not prime then we can just ignore them or remove them from our numbers while finding the prime numbers. This would be analogy to any of the daily life instances like we have to find out the Good guys from a large group of boys, so we can just check each boy that is he Good or Bad, or we can just remove the bad boys from the group of boys and we will be left with the Good boys.
 
  • The next possible challenge or question that would stuck his mind that how to remove that non-prime numbers from the bunch of numbers from which we have to find the prime number. Similar to the problem that can come in above example i.e. how to remove the bad boys from the bunch of boys.
 
  • Then he might have analyzed the non-prime numbers and would had find that they surely have some divisor which is present in that group of numbers. He might also had noticed that from all the divisors he get there is at least a prime number in that bunch of divisors. Then he might had found that in a sequence of numbers he get every multiple of a prime in that bunch of numbers. So then by taking the concept of marking the multiple of a number as useless (non-prime) we can be left with the useful (prime) numbers.
 
  • Then the solution would be pretty easy that just mark the multiples of all the numbers that are not explored (prime numbers) till now in sequence as explored & at same time store these unexplored numbers as these will be the prime numbers as till reaching it no number divided it.
