Algorithm Description: 

Genetic algorithms are inspired by Darwin's theory about evolution. Solution to a problem solved by genetic algorithms is evolved.

Algorithm is started with a set of solutions (represented by chromosomes) called population. Solutions from one population are taken and used to form a new population. This is motivated by a hope, that the new population will be better than the old one. Solutions which are selected to form new solutions (offspring) are selected according to their fitness - the more suitable they are the more chances they have to reproduce.

This is repeated until some condition (for example number of populations or improvement of the best solution) is satisfied.

 

Working towards the algorithm:

Genetic algorithms find applications in the field of artificial intelligence and robotics. I came across genetic algorithms while studying the course of artificial algorithms at JIIT. According to me the following things might have crossed the inventors mind:

  1. In robotics and artificial intelligence we try to stimulate the human behavior in a bot. Evolution is a   concept which has been used by the humans to thrive in a difficult environment. Thus, he might have studied the natural selection and tried to replicate it in his algorithm.
  2. There are certain situations faced by a programmer when he has very less knowledge of the problem. But he has been tasked to create a solution. Genetic algorithms are one of the best ways to solve a problem for which little is known. They are a very general algorithm and so will work well in any search space. So the inventors inspiration might have been developing a very general algorithm fit for any situation.
  3. Genetic algorithms tend to thrive in an environment in which there is a very large set of candidate solutions and in which the search space is uneven and has many hills and valleys. Thus   the inventor might have thought to provide a solution for the problem where there are many likely solutions by selecting the fit solution.
